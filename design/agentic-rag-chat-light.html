What is a checkpointer, and what is 12 × 9?
◆
rag_search calculator · 1 round

A checkpointer persists a thread's graph state as checkpoints, giving agents short-term memory across turns and enabling human-in-the-loop and time-travel[1]. You attach one when compiling the graph, e.g. graph.compile(checkpointer=...)[2].

And 12 × 9 = 108.

How do I connect the entry point of a graph in v1.0?
◆

In LangGraph v1.0 you connect the entry point with add_edge(START, "node_name"); the older set_entry_point() helper is deprecated[1]. A StateGraph defines nodes and edges over a shared state, and START / END are the virtual entry and exit nodes[2].
